### üü° MEDIUM: Conversion Tracking Validation (Priority 4)

**Issue:** Conversion rates of 237-7461% suggest all user actions are being counted as conversions.

**Why This Is Important:**
- Cost per conversion ($0.04) is suspiciously low vs. industry norm ($2-10)
- May indicate pixel is firing on page views, not actual purchases/leads
- Inflates performance metrics and masks true customer acquisition cost

**Technical Assessment:**
Looking at the data:
- Campaign #120214150916090515: 37,527% conversion rate (130 clicks ‚Üí 48,785 conversions)
- This means ~375 conversions per click (impossible for traditional conversion events)
- Likely explanation: Pixel firing on multiple page events (page view, scroll depth, time on site)

**Recommended Actions:**
1. **Audit Meta Pixel Configuration:**
   - Review Events Manager to see which events count as "conversions"
   - Separate into:
     - **Standard Events:** Purchase, Lead, CompleteRegistration (true conversions)
     - **Custom Events:** PageView, ViewContent (engagement metrics)

2. **Reconfigure Conversion Optimization:**
   - Set campaigns to optimize for "Purchase" or "Lead" events only
   - Move micro-conversions (page views) to engagement campaigns
   - Expected result: Conversion rate drops to 2-10%, cost per conversion rises to $1-5

3. **Implement Conversion Value Tracking:**
   - Add revenue values to Purchase events
   - Track ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) not just conversion volume
   - This will provide true ROI picture for business decisions
